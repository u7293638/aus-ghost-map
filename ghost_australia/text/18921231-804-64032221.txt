BOSH! Hey diddle diddle ! the cat and the fiddle Are getting old-fashioned, I trow ; More novel 'twould be for a great wallaby An organ to play with one toe. For the moon to give chase to the man on her face And the comet to join in the fun, For the evergi een rabbit to weep at the habit Of squatters infesting its run. For the novelist pale to yield up his tale To the sheep of Bo-Peep's woful verse. For the ghost of the corner to sit on Jack Horner While Coroner Cats seek the hearse. For small dogs to giggle when cows give a wriggle That lands them right over the moon, Is really quite stale, as is also the tale Of the dish making off with that spoon. Now not to go off with a spoon when there's moon Is a far more astonishing thing, And to see the moon giggle-oh, hey diddle-diddle In that case you really might sing. E.
