Dennis t. Heaton. .£400 AND COSTS. London, March 13.— Mr. Robert Dennis sued Mr. Henniker Seaton, M.P. for Canterbury, for £500 for services alleged to have been rendered. The plaintiff enumerated the services as consist ing of writing Mr. Heaton's speeches, his articles in the National Beview, and his letters to the Times and the Postmaster-General on postal mat terafrom 1885 to 1888; also his statement before the Colonial Conference, and for acting as what is known as Mr. Heaton's ghost. The action has been settled by the payment by the defendant of £400 and .£200 costs.
