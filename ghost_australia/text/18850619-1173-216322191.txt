A Heii on her Eggs. AH 1 ah! this time I've got, I think, just five, White as the moon upon an August night, I long to see the contents well alive, Eor those chicks, still unborn, are my delight. My eldest egg—now let me pause and see: He'll be a valiant rooster-bird, of course, Having the grace cf the ailantus tree, A linnet's voice, the brute strength of a horse My (second, I must very fondly dream, Will ba a poule de Uttres, and very wise; She in linguistics will be held supreme, And she will learn the idiom of the flieB. That third, delicious, speckled egg of mine Will bring me forth the handsomest of males, With military genius, I opine— A fowl the foe of garden slugs and snails. That other there—that dotted little dear— Will cause my poor maternal mind regret; For she will be, I positively fear, The waywaid Cleopatra of my set. But, ah ! the one that has a beauty mark Eight on the top, from Duty ne'er will quail; She, Christian-like, will suffer in the dark And be the chickens' Florence Nightingale. So saying, the hen clucked loudly in her joy, And waved her wings upon the unhatched But then appeared a stalwart poultry-boy.
