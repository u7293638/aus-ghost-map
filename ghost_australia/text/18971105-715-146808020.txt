WEEN A YOUNG MAN IS IN LOVE. A man is often in serious perplexity about a proposal. He loves a girl, and be longs to tell her so, and secure her'for his own; and yet be knows quite well he is not in a position to face papa upon a question of settlements, and so be baits between two opinions tormented with doubt. If he has absolutely no prospects whatever, there certainly seems little good proposing marriage It would be better to keep silence altogether were it not for a way men have of being unable to help showing their love for a^girl, and haunting her society, even when they are aware their prospects are hopeless. To show a girl you love her, and to haunt her footsteps, is, mostly, to make her care for you in return—at all events, there is danger of doing so. That being the case, a man is bound in honor to speak. He cannot win a girl's..love by showing her his, and then ride away and say nothing more. He must tell her how matters stand. If he has the|ghost of a pros pect he'must ask her whether she thinks it sufficient to wait for; but he should make it plain that he cannot consider himself ill-used if she thinks the waiting too long and too indefinite. If there is no prospect at all, he cannot ask for any promise. But he can tell her how happy it would have made him to have her for his wife, had fate been kinder. That will, at least, prevent her eating her heart out in the mortification of fancying she has given her love to him without being asked for it—a mortification greater to a woman than a man can have the least idea of. Without definite prospects no man has [ any right to bind a girl by an engagement..
