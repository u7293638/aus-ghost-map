ST. PETERS, GLENELG. On Wednesday evening the new sanctu- ary and vestries of St. Peter's Church, Glenelg, were consecrated by Bishop Har- mer. The sacred edifice was crowded, and the service was most impressive. Canon Green read Psalms lxxxiv. and cxxii., and the lessons were read by Archdeacon Morse and Dean Marryat respectively. The Ven. Archdeacon French, the esteemed ex- pastor of St. Peter's, was the preacher, and the choir, under Mr. W. B. Hills sang the anthem "Hosanna in the highest" and the "Te Deum Laudamus." The Registrar, having read the sentence of consecration, and the same having been ordered by the Bishop, with the petition and other deeds to be duly recorded and registered in the diocesan registry, Bishop Harmer said, "I declare the sanctuary and vestries of this Church of St. Peter to be now consecrated and set apart to God for ever, in the name of the Father, and. of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost."
