Jl ' J THE WAR IN NEW CALEDONIA. 1\. — I was not with the troops when the shot was fired, being more comfortably engaged. 2\. — Col Wendling has promised me " great events " shortly. I hope there is no real danger. — Capt. de Joux always exhibits a photograph of a settler's wife at Boula- pari, after mess, and we have great fun in drinking her health. 4\. — I forgot to mention that the spur of the mountain, upon which / nearly gave up the ghost, has been named after me. 5.— It is really very heart- rending to your correspondent not to be able to send home any reports of massacres and bloodshed wherewith to amuse your readers.
