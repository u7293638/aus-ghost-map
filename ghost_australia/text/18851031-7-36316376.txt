SPORTING. LATEST SPORTING. [By Telegraph.] [From our Special Reporter.] Melbourne, October 30. TO-DAY'S RACKS. Sixteen horses are left in the Maribyrnong Plate, the principal absentees being Philip Augustus and Fernandez. The former was beaten in a gallop by his stable companion Acme yesterday morning. A^good deal of Sydney and Melbourne money will go with this pair. The issue for the plate should lie between Acme and Blairgowrie, and in this hot, sultry weather the colt should be the more reliable of the two. Hill's colt, Telephone, will run well, and has more than an outside show, as he beat Micawber easily yesterday. Trojan, Halcyon, and Damocles are all good ones, and Essie is a very nice filly. The Derby is still a very open race, opinions being greatly divided here amongst all, but the backers of Nordenfeldt. These won't budge from the opinion that this colt must win, while the Thunderbolt party and Dunlop followers are equally sanguine. John Hill affirms that Isonomy will do them all, and last night he was backed again by the stable, so that a grand race should ensue, especially as the chances of Remus are favorably regarded, and last year's running must not be forgotten, when the outsider, Ruf us, won. The weather is worse than a bad summer's day in Adelaide, the dust is flying in all directions, but as the glass is going down it may be cool to-morrow.
