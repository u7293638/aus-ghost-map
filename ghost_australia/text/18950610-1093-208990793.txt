PERSONATING A GHOST. A sensational story was tola the jtsaLlarat East police on Wednesday night by Mr. Frederick Park, a resident of Cobden- street, Mount Pleasant. Mr. Park stated that when at the junction of Eord and Princes streets at about half-past 7 o'clock he heard the screams of a woman. On going to ascertain the cause of the noise lie observed a man dressed in white, and whose face was besmeared with phosphorus. The "ghost" carried a piece of timber cut in the shape of a coffin-lid. Mr. Park says he went in pursuit of the man, and catching him a scuffle ensued. The man drew a knife, struck Mr. Park on the arm, cutting his coat and shirt, and scratching his arm, and then made off. Constable McPherson on Thursday took the matter in hand, and made close enquiries of the neighbors as to whether they heard screams at the spot and hour mentioned above. Mrs. A. Harvey, _who resides at the corner of Eord and Princes streets, and at whose gate the assault is alleged to have , been committed, stated that she was at home at the hour mentioned and did not hear any screams, whilst two other residents of Princes-street and a pedestrian, who happened to pass down Eureka-street, near Princes-street", at about half-past 7 o'clock, did not hear any noise. On the other hand, four persons (according to the Courier), including a young girl, heard faint screams. The police desire the woman who was molested to call at the lock-up and give information.
