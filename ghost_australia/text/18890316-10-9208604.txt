MR. HENNIKER HEATON IN THE LAW COURTS. . ! Mr. Robert Dennys, the well-known ship- owner, has entered a libel action against Mr. Henniker-Heaton, M P. for Canterbury, damages being laid at £500. The alleged libel was contained in articles printed in the National Review and The Times, and letters to the Postmaster-General during 1885 and 1888 in connection with the Australian mail service, in which Mr. Dennys was personally alluded to as " the Ghost" which frightened shippers from effecting reforms. The case has been settled by defendant paying £400 and costs. " ' ,
