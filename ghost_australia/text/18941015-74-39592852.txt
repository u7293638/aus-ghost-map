LAWRENCE VALE. Anniversary services in connection with the Wesleyan Church, Lawrence Vale, were held yesterday morning and evening. The Rev. D. J. Flockart conducted the morning service, and delivered an interest. ing discourse on the subject " Stephen, the first maltyr," whom he described as a man full of faith and the Holy Ghost. In the evening the Rev. W. Shaw preached on the text, "This is af sithful saying, and worthy of all acceptation, that Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners, of whom I am chief," (I. Tim., i., 15). There was a large congregation, and the service was iLtorosting throughout. Special hymns were sung by the choir, Mr J. Peter officiating as leader and Mrs Millard as organist.
