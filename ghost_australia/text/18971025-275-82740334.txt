HAUNTED. CHAPTER I WAS IT A GHOST. Delia drew her to the window, and indicated the precise spot where she had observed the figure. "Tell me," she said, "how old would my brother James be if he had lived? He was much older than Reggy or myself." "He'd a been nigh upon forty. But there! good-night, Miss Delia or I shall talk till dawn." Delia returned her good-night kindly, and ere she laid her head on her pillow, once more drew aside her curtain, and gazed out on the enchanting prospect of snow, lighted by the rays of the moon. What a start she gave as she gazed ! Was it fancy, or was there actually the dim figure of a man, wearing a slouch hat, standing out there amid the snow- wreaths?
