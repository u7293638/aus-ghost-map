The Wrong Foot. "Dat eands dat," said a colored man who was brought into the central sta tion the other day as a suspicious char acter. "What?" asked the sergeant. "Dis yore," was the reply as he pro. duced a rabbit's foot and tossed it con temptuously on the desk. "Dat ar' de off hind fut of a rabbit." "What is it good for?" "Nuflin 'tall." "What do you carry it fort" "Bought dat fut of an old Kentucky nigger, who said it would keep do ghosts off." "Well, hasn't it?" "Yes, do ghosts haln't come, but do purliceman has. Walked right up an' grabbed me 'fore I knowed it. Tat's do good to keep do ghosts off an' let do coppers grab ye? Doan' believe in do rabbit's foot no mo'. You jist keep it. Dean' want nuflin in my pocket dat walks me in yore an' sends me up fur six months."-Detroit Free Press.
