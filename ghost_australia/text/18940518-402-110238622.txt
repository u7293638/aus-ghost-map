APHORISMS. The influences that go into us in boyhood fashion the experience that we go into in manhood.—Carlos Martyn. A journalist is a grumbler, a censurer, a giver of advice, a regent of sovereigns, a tutor of nations. Four hostile newspapers are more to be feared than a thousand bay- onets.—Napoleon. Open biographical volumes where you will, and the man who has no faith in religion has faith in the nightmare and in ghosts.— Bulwer. The best way to keep good acts in memory is to refresh them with new. —Cato. It is a mistake to suppose that men succeed through success ; they much oftener succeed through failure.—S. Smiles. Many mean things are done in the family for which moods are put forward as the excuse, when the moods themselves are the most inexcusable things of all. A man or woman in tolerable health has no mortal right to indulge in an unpleasant mood.— J. G. Holland . There is one body who is wiser than any body and that is everybody.—Talleyrand. They that believe have Christ in their hearts, heaven in their eyes, and the world under their feet. God's spirit is their guide. His word their rule, His fear their guard, His people their companions, His promises their cordials, holiness their way, and heaven their home.—John Newton.
