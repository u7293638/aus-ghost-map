TALLY HO The garrison harriers met again at Brown's River on Tuesday last, but as the moon was mute as far as advertisement might go, the field was composed of none but those who were admitted to the unaccountable secret. The back, of course, went away as before, but lost his pursuers, ere they lost him in the echoing confines of Proctor's Gully. As, however, it is reported by a thoughtful visitor at a former event, that one of the dams has a bleating little fawn at her foot too young to struggle its way out of difficulty, I feel that it is not further necessary to suggest to Squire Baynton the humane dictate of barring the cry of the hounds at Huntingfield, until full growth is afforded to the came by the coming season.
