If the inquisitive and courteous reader will walk any very dark night in a southerly direc tion from the northern end of Guy-street, and keep an attentive look-out, he will see in hazy shadow the mysterious object above described, which, on a clear night, will assume the shape of a newly, white-washed chimney in the gable end of the cottage opposite to Mr. Grob's house, and which is all that is left of what may now be termed the ghost of guy-street, Glen Innes.
