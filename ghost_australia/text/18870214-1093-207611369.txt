VICTORIA AMATEUR TURF CLUB MEETING. [By Telegraph.] Melbourne. February 12, The Victoria Amateur Turf Club held a most successful meeting at Caulfield on Saturday. The weather was tine but hot, the attendance i being good, including his Excellency . the Governor. The various events were well con tested, the racing being of a first-class description. Ariel won the Selling Race, followed home by Liddington and The Ghost. The winner was bought in for £150, Nineteen started for the Oakleigh Handicap, which was won in fine style by the favorite, Surprise, who beat Master John by a head only; Chicago was third. Louis appropriated the Hurdle Race by a short head after a grand struggle with Spartan, who was followed by Encore. Tho Criterion Stakes, for two-year-olds, fell to Albert Loddon, who defeated the top weight, Bedouin, by a long neck, Wilara being third only half a length behind. Goodwood had an easy victory in the Steeplechase, his immediate followers being Roulette and Stud ley. Four horses came to grief over the first fence, and McGowan and Cox, jun., two of the jockeye, were hurt, The Hawkesbury Handi cap resulted in a splendid victory for Britisher, who beat King of the West, Lesbia, and seven others.
