CHARITY CONCERT. An entertainment in aid of a ch an able object was given in the School of Arts, Burwood, last night, under the auspices of the Loyal Hand of Friendship Lodge, Miss O.P., and the Burwood Labour League. The first part of the programme consisted of a minstrel entertainment, terminating in a farce entitled "Ghosts." In the second part vocal items were rendered by the Misses Flood and Prentice (two), and Messrs. J. Pickles, P. J. Spencer, and A. XICUOIIF. Mr. H. Janson played an overture, and Mrs. Jackson gave an Irish song and dance. Miss L. Nicholls accompanied on the piano. An amusing farce, entitled "The Dancing Maniac," concluded the entertainment, which was largely attended.
