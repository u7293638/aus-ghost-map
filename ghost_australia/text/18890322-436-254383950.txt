ACTION AGAINST~MR. HENNIKER HEATON. LONDON, March 13. An action has been brought by Mr. Robert Dennis against Mr. J. Henniker Heaton, the Conservative, member for Canterbury. The plaintiff claimed £500 for services rendered, and he alleged that, acting in the capacity of private secretary to Mr. Heaton between 1885 and 1888, he had written for him his speeches, certain articles in tbe National Review, letters published in The Times aud others addressed to the Postmastor-General, and the statement made by MT. Heaton before the Colonial Conference held in London in 1887.' He asserted that he had in fact throughout been that gentleman' s ghost. Tbe case has been settled, Mr. Heaton paying £400 to the plaintiff for his services and the coBts of his action, amounting to £200.
