CHRIST CHURCH, NORTH SHORE. ? THE foundation stone of a new church, for the Anglican residents of the parish of Willoughby, on the North Shore, was laid yesterday afternoon, by Dr Barker, the Motrapo plan Bishop, in the presence of a large body of clergymen and many hundred lay members of the Church of England A considerable number of the clergy and laity came over from the Sydney side in a steamer, which landed them at the bottom of the recently completed stone steps at the head of Lavender Bay, and in the immediate vicinity of the intended church The edifice to be known as Christ Church is to stand on a comparatively narrow ledge rather more than half way up the steep declivity at the head of the bay-a site at once convenient and picturesque, commanding a truly magnificent view of the harbour, the city, and its surrounding neighbourhood By means of excellent roads, already judiciously laid out and constructed, easy access will be had to this church from every portion of that populous section of the parish of Willoughby, for which the structure is principally designed From the elevated and rugged nature of the ground a somewhat large amount must have been expended on the foundation which has been brought up to the floor line, the foundations resting on the solid rock The "corner stone" laid yesterday was found by the visitors to be a block of about one ton weight, the place in which it was fixed (with all proper formalities) being immediately under the oriel window of the chancel apse.
