"One natural verse"-that is what Mr. Stevenson has given us, and it is the natural ness of what he writes that is its leading quality. His poems are the straightforward utterance of a thoroughly sane and, at the same time, exqusitely sensitive mind. It cannot be said test he has been uninfluenced by the other poetry of the time, for he is a man of letters who has read widely as he has felt deeply; but he is entirely free going after strange rhymes and other torturinge of speech which is the fault of so many of the distinguished poets of the present day.
