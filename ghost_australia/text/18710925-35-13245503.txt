CHAPEL-OF-EASE AT NORTH WILLOUGHBY.- Shortly before 4 o'clock on last Saturday afternoon, the ceremony of laying the foundation stone of a chapel- of-ease \- to be known as St. Stephen's - was performed at North Willoughby by Mrs. W. Cave-Brown-Dave, in the presence of a large number of residents of the vicinity and of visitors from Sydney, who, in spite of the inclement aspect of the weather, had made a visit to the spot, to which they were conveyed from the Milson's Point ferry via the new Flat Rock bridge. Although the roads are now much better than formerly, the slight rain had at places made them rather heavy. They were, however, so far passable that no accident occurred. The ceremony was to have taken place at 3 o'clock in the afternoon but it was postponed till about an hour afterwards when the rain showed signs of clearing- off. After the favourite hymn beginning "All hail the power of Jesu's name" had been sung, prayer was offered by the Rev. W. Cave-Brown-Cave. Mr. H. H. Bligh, Mayor of the Borough of North Willoughby, then presented Mrs. Cave with a handsome mallet and trowel, the former bearing an appropriate inscription on silver, and said by Mr Bligh to be the handiwork of one of the members of the congregation. Mr. Bligh then placed in the cavity of the stone a bottle, which he said contained a copy of the Sydney Morning Herald, ca copy of the Austra- lian Churchman, a few coins of the realm, and a parch- ment scroll, of which the following is a copy - "North Willoughby To the glory of God, and in remembrance of the Christian deacon, the martyr St. Stephen; the foundation stone of the mission Church was laid on Sep- tember the twenty-third, in the year of our Lord One thousand eight hundred and seventy one, by Mrs. Cave- Brown-Cave. The Rev. W. C. Cave-Brown-Cave M.A. incumbent of the parish , the Right Rev F. Barker D. D., Bishop of the diocese; trustees - the Rev. W.C.,B.Cave, H.H. Bligh, and R. Seldon. Committee Messrs Bligh, M'Lonnon, Harnott, Peddleston, Seldon Kennard, Stuck, Osborne. Builder of stonework, Mr' John Jago; secretary and treasurer, Mr. H. H. Bligh" As soon as the stone had been lowered into the bed pre- pared for its reception, it was declared by Mrs. Cave to be well and truly laid. The Dean of Sydney then addressed the assemblage. His allusions were more particularly directed to the impressing upon the minds of his bearers the importance of the work that had just been inaugurated, its the result of which there would he provision for making known the blessings of the Gospel to some who might otherwise remain unacquainted with them, and continue to be unobservers of the day of rest. The Rev. Mr. Cave, who followed, detailed a few of the circum- stances that had led to the initiation of this important work. He prayed that God's blessing would rest upon those who had vigen their assistance to this this movement, upon that which might belong them, as well as upon those whose duty it might be to labour in this house of worship. "The Son of God goes forth to war" was next sung, and the benediction pronounced by Dean Cowper brought the first part of the proceedings to a close. After meeting in a rural marquee, erected close by, followed, The tea, and all the usual concomitants, provided abun- dantly by the ladies of the neighbourhood, were dispensed under their supervision.
