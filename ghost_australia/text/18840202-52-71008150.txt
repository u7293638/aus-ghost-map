Brennan, Messrs Coreans, Cheveraune, J. Sebe, Binthiaux. Fears are entertained of the total loss of the schooner Wonga Wonga, with all hands. She left Nambuccra River at noon on January 16, in company with the schooner Mar- garet Jane, and has not since been heard of. The captain of the Margaret Jane reports having met with a heavy squall from the southward on the night of January 17, and he bore up for Port Stephens, which he reached in safety, and at the time of the squall the Wonga, judging from her position, would have been off Newcastle Bight ; but nothing has since been heard of her. The Wonga was the property of Mr. Thomas Davis, of Brisbane Water, and was timber laden. The crew on board were—Henry Rossander, mas- ter ; Charles Peterson, mate ; William Oberg, AB. ; and another man as cook whose name is unknown. She was 56 tons register, and aa neither vessel or cargo were insured, the total loss will be about £1000.
