CRITERION THEATRE. There was a crowded attendance at the Criterion Theatre last night, the best proof of the public appreciation of Mr Alfred Dampier's artistic enterprise in giving one performance a week of a Shakespearian drama No little trouble is involved by the change, not only in study, but in the dislocation of the regular scenic arrangements at the theatre, so that the success of " Hamlet " last night is all the more welcome Mr. Alfred Dampier had the audience with him throughout the evening, and especially during his touching interpretation of the scene with the Ghost This evening " The Green Lanes of England " will again be staged, with Mr Dampier in a favourite character part as Gentleman George
