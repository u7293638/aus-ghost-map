PUNCTUATION. TO THE EDITOR OF THE HERALD. Sir-Now that unfavourable criticism on University spelling. Post Office carvings, etc, may be said to be the order of the day, allow me to say a word on punctuation. In one of our principal streets, not quite half a league from Haymarket, there stands a sightly Clock of buildings, the name of which ends in SS, and to mark the possessive case on apostrophe or third S is added, thereby causing quite a I curiosity in the neighbourhood. Not perhaps, so much on account of the new grammatical production, as the fact that according to report the ghost of Lindley Murray is nightly I seen hovering about the locality, gazing in wonderment at the scholarly work of art, I am, c, JOHN SCANLAN.
