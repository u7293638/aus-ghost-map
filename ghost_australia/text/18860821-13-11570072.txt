SPORTING INTELLIGENCE. VICTORIA AMATEUR TURF CLUB MEETING. The Caulfield Grand National Steeplechase meeting to day, after thehea\yrain of Thursday and 1 Friday, looked an impossibility last night, but the natural surface of the ground is of such a favourable nature as to insure the racing coming as originally announced. It is a notable fact that the committee of the Victoria Amateur Turf Club have never yet been reduced to the necessity of postponing a meeting through stress of weather, and it is hardly likely they will do HO on the present occasion. However, Mr. Bond has summoned the committee for half-past 10 o'clock this morning, and due notice of the final decision will be conveyed to sporting resorts without delay. The first race, the Sapling Stakes, is timed for half-past 1, and the Hurdle Race and Steeplechase are respectively set down for half-past 2 and a quarter to 4, whilst the Balaclava Stakes, the last race on the card, will be started at half past i. Special trains leave Flinders-street at frequent intervals from 11.43 team. to 1.50 p.m., returning as usual after the last race. Ordinary trains also leave Caulfield for Prince's-bridge at 4.14 and 4.20, calling at all stations except Richmond. The clubs were unusually quiet last night, and the uncertainty of the meeting coming off, coupled with the presumed heavy going, nearly stopped speculation, for the Hurdle Race, 500 to 250 was taken about Huntsman, as also 100 to 30 Ocean and wanted ; whilst Bonnie Lad was supported to a little money at 100 to 11 ; otherwise the betting was 10 to 1 bar three. The Steeplechase was a dead letter, and 100 to 20 on the field for the double event was unheeded.
